Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a beloved Filipino stew known for its rich, nutty peanut sauce and tender oxtail or beef. It’s often served during special occasions and family gatherings. The dish combines slow-cooked meat with vegetables like banana blossoms, string beans, and eggplant, all simmered in a savory peanut-based sauce thickened with toasted rice flour.

The key to a good Kare-Kare is patience—slowly simmering the meat until tender, and letting the peanut sauce embrace every ingredient with its rich, nutty flavor.

Ingredients:

  • OxTail: 2 lbs
  • Peanut Butter: 1 cup
  • Ground Rice Flour: 1/4 cup
  • Annatto Seeds: 2 tbsp
  • Cooking Oil: 2 tbsp
  • Garlic: 2 cloves
  • Onion: 1 medium size
  • Beef Broth: 2 Cups
  • Fish Sauce: 1 tsp
  • Banana Blossom: 1 medium size
  • Eggplant: 1 medium size
  • String Beans: 1 bunch
  • Pechay: 1 bunch
  • Salt and Pepper: to taste

Preparation:

  1. Simmer the oxtail until tender
  2. Add peanut butter, annatto water, and broth.
  3. Stir in toasted rice flour to thicken.
  4. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper.
  5. Blanch banana blossom, eggplant, beans, and pechay.
  6. Combine meat, sauce, and vegetables.
  7. Garnish with bagoong on the side and serve hot.

Cooking Tips:

  • Toast the rice flour slowly over low heat until lightly golden—this brings out a nutty aroma that deepens the flavor of the peanut sauce
